Manifesto

I truly loathe the futility of talking about my art, so I'll let my mind go as abstract as it wishes. The joke is on you as much as it is on me.
Perhaps the most effective introduction to my art and even to myself, which I realised not long ago, is to state that I have felt gaps within reality for as long as I can remember.
People around me always seemed to possess pieces of a puzzle which I did not even know were there; as though they were aware and familiar with certain facets of being alive that had eluded me, and to this day, I struggle to grasp within my reach as though they were more complete than I could even fathom to understand; so widespread were these pieces of information that I was left feeling somewhat defective, living in stasis at the bottom of a vast body of murky water.
From there, godless, isolated, stupid, and crooked, I consciously and subconsciously use artmaking to try to explain and process reality, repeating those ancient human acts of storytelling and theorising to make sense of the world. I tell fairytales. I hypothesise theories.
I am designing cosmogonies.
As such, my art is shaped by two forces pulling in opposite directions: a desire to create and a desire to destroy. The rapture between these two polarities generates fertile ground on which I feel I can walk further and further into life, towards freedom, whatever that may be.
I want more than just to survive or live; I want to thrive! Art's functions in this quest will be of aid and testimony: each piece reminds me that I am trying to move somewhere.
